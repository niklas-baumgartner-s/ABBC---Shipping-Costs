ABOUT OUR CERAMICS

Drawing

With our artists creative ideas, we design our products out of clay. All from delicate porcelain to hefty sculptural art and everything in between.
The possibilities and variants are limitless in designing our ceramic products. 

Making

As we start working with the suitable clay, it first gets sloppier and wetter, before it begins to dry in the atmosphere. 
Depending on how our creative artists decorate our ceramics, there are different techniques to start the crafting the art. 

Drying

After the two steps, our ceramic products will be placed on a rack indoors or outdoors depending on the weather. We let out product airdry - meaning it will not be covered. Drying is the process that takes the longest and also one of the important process, as cracks as results are not uncommon when firing. The reason for this is, when the product has not dried up enough before the firing and glazing process. 

Trimming and Cleaning up

Once our ceramics dry up, we call this "greenware" and it is as it's most fragile. Our experienced artists keep everything gentle and work delicately. We use various of tools starting from a piece of fly wire to rub the work all over to knife or metal kidney for more dynamic treatment. 

Bisque Firing and Glazing

Once our products are bone dry after the final shaping, the process of "firing" can begin. The bisque kiln is fired to 1000Â°C. This burns off the water in the clay and takes about 10 hours to complete the cycle, and about two days to cool down. 
Depending on our planned design, these products will be glazed over. We use different ways to apply the glaze - Dipping, Spraying, Brushing, and Pouring. 
After the glaze process, the products will be set in the kiln for a glaze fire, which is the last step to complete the process of our ABBC ceramic products.
